Politics & Local Government

Overview

The Native Title Act 1993 “recognises the rights and interests of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people in land and waters according to their traditional laws and customs’’. Native Title allows the right to negotiate certain activities on the land and water and how it is managed, rather than commercial ownership. Each state has additional land acts and tenure regimes that recognise Inidgenous rights to their traditional lands and water, often offering greater rights and control. Prescribed Body Corporates are established for management, representation and decision-making purposes under the Native Title Act, usually reflecting traditional clans, family groupings or decision making structures. Sometimes a council of elders provide guidance in line with traditional decision making protocols.

The “Cape York Peninsula Aboriginal Land” is unique to Cape York. This land is managed jointly by Traditional Owners and government departments through Indigenous Management Agreements to achieve conservation outcomes. Legislation such as the Aboriginal Land Act 1991 and the Cape York Peninsula Heritage Act 2007, recognises Traditional Owners land rights and began returning the land across Cape York. The Australian government has been purchasing existing pastoral leases (i.e. cattle stations) and negotiating with the Traditional Owners how land can be allocated and managed in line with conservation goals and requirements. The Cape York Tenure Resolution Programme sets out the current state of land tenure and environmental management on the Cape York Peninsula, through Indigenous Land Use Agreements. There are multiple Indigenous shire councils across Cape York, one for each discrete community. These councils hold land in the mostly former missions in trust under a “Deed of Grant in Trust” title. These democratically elected councils administer a range of collective services from housing to essential services within the discrete communities themselves but rarely to homelands and outstations.
